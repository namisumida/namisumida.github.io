About me

I am a data visualization developer and designer. This site is home to my personal and professional work.

Currently, I am working as a Research Analyst at the Pew Research Center's Journalism & Media project, conducting social science research on journalism and the news media.

Data Visualizations

  • Which countries drink the most?

    A ranking of countries based on the amount of beer, wine, and spirits that an average person in each country consumes in a year.

  • Standards of Living

    Using data from the World Bank, I compared several health metrics in the US and some of the least developed countries in the world to better understand how living standards can widely vary around the world.

  • Nexus Blitz Jungle Duos

    A tool to explore win rates of jungler pairs in the League of Legends Nexus Blitz game mode.

    Part of a series of data visualizations created in collaboration with Doran's Lab.

  • Paths to the House of Representatives

    A look at past experiences of the members of the House of Representatives, including their education, prior careers, and previously-held political offices.

  • Nexus Blitz Play and Win Rates

    A dashboard of play and win rates of three League of Legends game modes.

    Part of a series of data visualizations created in collaboration with Doran's Lab.

  • Ultimate frisbee: Does the game differ by division?

    A comparison of throw types, success rates, offensive and defensive efficiency in competitive Men's, Women's, and Mixed ultimate frisbee.

  • Cable TV news coverage of the Ford-Kavanaugh hearings

    A look at what MSNBC, CNN, and the Fox News Channel chose to display in their lower-third chyrons.

Reports

  • News Media Attitudes in France

    A deep dive into news media attitudes and habits in France, revealing a broad discontent among the French toward the news media.

  • For Local News, Americans Embrace Digital but Still Want Strong Community Connection

    A study of nearly 35,000 U.S. adults on local news habits and attitudes toward their local news media.

  • Explore local news habits in your city with our new interactive

    A blog post explaining how to use an online, interactive feature that allows users to explore local news habits in one's metro area.

  • Social Media Bots Draw Public's Attention and Concern

    A look at the public's opinion about social media bots and their impact on how people stay informed about current events.

  • Distinguishing Between Factual and Opinion Statements in the News

    An experiment testing Americans' ability to categorize news statements as factual or opinion statements, and how news source attribution and left/right political appeal affect their ability to do so.
